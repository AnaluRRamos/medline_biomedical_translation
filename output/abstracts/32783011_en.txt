Occupational risk factors are associated with many types of neoplasms including cervical cancer. To review the specialized literature for evidence on the relationship between cervical cancer and exposure to occupational hazards. Literature search in electronic databases using keywords cervical cancer and occupational risk. Workers occupationally exposed to tobacco, fungi or bacteria, metalworking fluids and tetrachloroethylene used in dry cleaning and for metal degreasing exhibited higher susceptibility to cervical cancer. Few studies sought to investigate relationships between cervical cancer and occupational hazards, which hinders the attempts at establishing a causal link.
