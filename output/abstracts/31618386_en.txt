Results were analyzed descriptively, in five analytical categories. Fifteen articles published on national and international journals were selected, with predominance of experimental studies that tested the effects of such technologies. The types of educational technology developed were printed materials, software and video, as well as mock-up and telephone support. Falls in the elderly were the most discussed theme. The studies have shown that the types of technology found are feasible to promote health education for the community elderly. The technologies developed to promote health education for the elderly were multiple and proved effective for use in community interventions.
